This is where it starts:
a blank page,
a blinking cursor,
the whole world waiting
to be written into being.

I have always been building.
First with numbers on a chalkboard,
then with tables in a warehouse,
now with words on a screen
that only I can see
until I push.

Every poem is a deployment.
Every line, a migration.
And the fear is always the same:
will it run?
will it land?
will someone read it
and know what I meant?

I press enter.
The world receives.
